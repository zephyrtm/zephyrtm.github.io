## humble beginnings
back in 2024, i first discovered the magical land that is the Linux family of operating systems. it was when i bought a crappy second-hand laptop on my local equivalent of ebay with the prospect of fixing it. upon receiving it and turning it on, besides learning i probably should have cleaned out the fans first, i also noticed this was the previous owner's school-managed laptop, and while i could have wiped the restricted copy of windows that was on it, i decided it would be more fun to see what i could do to jailbreak it.

after some research, i stumbled upon a tool by the name of [chntpw](https://chntpw.com) (Offline Windows Password & Registry Editor), which seemed to be able to fulfil the task of blanking the administrator password, and noticed that it was a Linux-only command line application. it was then that i first installed ubuntu, or rather used it from within the live environment (remember, i didn't know much more about Linux than its name) and after a few attempts, succeeded in blanking the administrator password. as i was erasing all of that work with a fresh copy of windows 10, i remembered that other operating system i had used on the way, ubuntu. at that moment, i forced shutdown on the laptop, breaking the windows 10 installation process beyond repair.

i decided that this 11.6″ quad-core 8gb RAM-having piece of oem-ware would be my experimentation machine from that moment onward.

## the os frenzy
from that point on, Linux became an obsession of mine. at any given waking moment, i was either installing a new distro, ricing said distro, or daydreaming about items 0 and 1. I tried, in order, 
- [ubuntu](https://ubuntu.com)
- [Linux Mint](https://linuxmint.com)
- [Pop!_OS](https://system76.com/pop/)
- [VanillaOS](https://vanillaos.org)
- [Garuda Linux](https://garudalinux.org)
- [EndeavourOS](https://endeavouros.com)
- [CachyOS](https://cachyos.org)
- [Manjaro](https://manjaro.org)
- [Archcraft](https://archcraft.io)
- [Arch Linux](https://archlinux.org)

... and i'm still on Arch today. with this list, i especially want to highlight how quickly all of this went. all these switches and rices were within the span of just under two months, which is incredibly fast. Each time, there was something I craved. ubuntu? speed and customization. Garuda and especially Manjaro? better battery life. CachyOS? stability. There was something about Arch-based distros that i really loved, that i couldn't find on anything else. i liked Archcraft so much that i even tried to install it on my main PC and start dualbooting, but the installer didn't work. i wanted to report it as an issue to the developer team, until i realized it was made by one person and decided to keep looking for something else. important to note here is that i believed i couldn't handle Arch itself, because of several videos i'd seen on youtube, until i saw one by the creator Bog in which he mentions an 'easy way' of installing Arch. I immediately put down my dinner to get the test laptop and my good ol' flash drive. Then I started the download on the ISO from archlinux.org, and could barely contain my excitement. after checking the video again i learned the command was called 'archinstall'. when it had finished flashing to the drive, i plugged it into the test laptop and booted from it. running archinstall and configuring my installation was a breeze, and i nearly didn't remember to finish my meal while the installation happened. it went off without a hitch.

## the Arch bliss
Arch was as great as i had imagined it to be, and there is another, but less interesting story as to how i found my current desktop environment. maybe sometime i'll tell it, but that time is not now. possibly that same day i'll upload my configs and links for all the stuff i use, because i especially love my sddm theme and waybar config.
